# Bloom's StAIrcase

**An openly licensed interactive tool helping educators design AI-integrated classroom activities.**

🔗 **[Use it now →](https://bakerxn.github.io/blooms-stAIrcase/index.html)**

## The Problem

Faculty across disciplines want to bring generative AI into their teaching, but many feel overwhelmed by the technology and unsure where to start. They need a structured way to brainstorm classroom activities that align with their specific learning objectives.

## The Solution

Bloom's StAIrcase is an interactive web tool built from [Rex Heer's](https://iastate.app.box.com/s/z0otio95lflaii1l2ro3h42kp8q6fdmm) depiction of the **knowledge dimensions** and **cognitive process dimensions** from Bloom's Revised Taxonomy. Educators click on any intersection in the grid to discover curated example activities for teaching about or with generative AI.

Every activity is tagged with:
- **Discipline** — STEM, Arts, or Humanities
- **AI Literacy Frame** — based on [Dr. Kara Kennedy's AI Literacy Framework](https://ailiteracy.institute/ai-literacy-and-ai-literacy-framework/), which maps AI skills onto the UNESCO Digital Literacy Global Framework

Users can also browse activities by:
- 📋 [All Activities](https://bakerxn.github.io/blooms-stAIrcase/all-activities.html) — the full list, filterable by tags
- 🏛️ [Discipline](https://bakerxn.github.io/blooms-stAIrcase/activities-by-discipline.html) — STEM, Arts, or Humanities
- 🧠 [AI Literacy Frame](https://bakerxn.github.io/blooms-stAIrcase/ai-literacy-framework.html) — Hardware & Software, Safety, Content Creation, and more

## Who It's For

Educatorsin higher education who want to integrate generative AI into their courses in a pedagogically grounded way — whether they're AI-curious beginners or experienced practitioners looking for fresh ideas.
